About FC Barcelona Femení

FC Barcelona Femení is the women’s football section of FC Barcelona, officially founded in 1988. Initially competing in local Catalan leagues, the team rose through the ranks to become one of the most dominant forces in European women’s football.

The club has been instrumental in developing women’s football in Spain and beyond, producing some of the sport’s brightest stars such as Alexia Putellas and Aitana Bonmatí. Known for their dynamic, possession-based style, the team embodies the same “tiki-taka” philosophy that defines FC Barcelona’s football identity.

Barcelona Femení made history in 2021 by winning their first UEFA Women’s Champions League, defeating Chelsea 4–0 in the final. Since then, they have continued to dominate both domestically and internationally, setting records for goals, attendance, and consistency.

Honors

  • UEFA Women’s Champions League: 3 titles (2021, 2023, 2024)
  • Primera División: 9 titles (2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2020–2025)
  • Copa de la Reina: 10 titles
  • Supercopa de España: 4 titles
  • Catalan Cup: 7 titles

The team’s home matches are played at the Estadi Johan Cruyff, a state-of-the-art stadium dedicated to the women’s and youth teams. FC Barcelona Femení continues to inspire a new generation of football fans and stands as a symbol of excellence and equality in sport.
